Step Count Sync Troubleshooting

If your step count in our app doesn't match what you see in Google Fit, there are several possible reasons:

1. Sync Your Google Fit App

Make sure your Google Fit app is up-to-date and has synced recently:

  1. Open the Google Fit app on your phone
  2. Pull down on the main screen to trigger a manual sync
  3. Wait for sync to complete (usually 30 seconds to a minute)

2. Data Synchronization Delay

There's often a delay between when steps are recorded on your device and when they become available through Google's API:

  • Steps you just took in the last few minutes may not appear immediately
  • Google's servers may update data in batches (every 15-30 minutes)
  • Our app already tries to get the freshest data possible with each update

3. Multiple Data Sources

Google Fit combines data from multiple sources:

  • Your phone's motion sensors
  • Connected fitness devices (watches, fitness trackers)
  • Manually entered activities
  • Third-party fitness apps

Our app already checks all possible data sources to ensure we're showing all your steps.

Time Zone Considerations

Our app uses your local time zone to calculate "today's" steps. If there's a mismatch between your device time zone and your current time zone, this could affect step counts.

Try These Solutions

  1. Sync your Google Fit app manually
  2. Wait a few minutes for data to propagate
  3. Try updating your step count again
  4. Check our "View detailed step data sources" page to see exactly which data sources we can access
  5. Make sure your phone's time and time zone are set correctly

If you continue to see discrepancies, this may be due to limitations in the Google Fitness API and how it makes data available to third-party apps.
